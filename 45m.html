Fossil Preservation and Research


Decoding a 45 million year old crocodilian

We have recently been asked to help preserve and research fossilized remains of a Crocodyliform from the early Eocene, found near the Great Rann of Kutch.

This specimen is estimated to have been 8-10 meters, almost as large as a Sarcosuchus Imperator, with the head extending to almost 2 meters.

The species is currently indeterminate and further research is on going. There is a high likelihood based on morphology and location, that it may have been the ancestor of the Gharials found in the nearby Indus valley.


The fossils are currently on display at the Bharatiya Sanskruti Darshan Museum, near the Collector Office in Bhuj.
