Fossil Preservation and Research


Decoding a 45 million year old crocodilian

We have recently been asked to help preserve and research fossilized remains of a Crocodyliform from the early Eocene, found near the Great Rann of Kutch.

This specimen, lovingly named "BLANK", is estimated to have been 8-10 meters, almost as large as a Sarcosuchus Imperator, with the head extending to almost 2 meters.

BLANK's species is currently indeterminate and further research is on going. There is a high likelyhood based on morphology and location, that it may have been the ancestor of the Gharials found in the nearby Indus valley.
